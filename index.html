WELCOME HERE!

I am a second-year Biomedical Engineering master student at Carnegie Mellon University advised by Prof. Tai-Sing Lee in the computer science department and CNBC (Center for Neural Basis of Cognition). I am passionate about uncovering the fundamental principle/mechanism behind complex systems, particularly in neuroscience. My interests lie in exploring the theoretical and computational principles of neural dynamics and population neural coding. Aside from that, I also have a strong affinity for physics and mathematics. My ultimate goal is to harmonize these diverse disciplines to address challenges of neural complex systems. Currently, I am actively seeking a computational neuroscience PhD position for 2024 fall.

CURRENTLY WORK ON...

I currently works on Exploring the neural circuit plasticity in the early visual cortex as a result of familiarity learning with video stimulus by analyzing neural data and building computational models. This research seeks to answer whether neural connectivity in the early visual cortex can encode spatial temporal memories with horizontal connections, leading to familiarity suppression.

MORE BACKGROUND...

I have a genuine passion for physics, and I've enjoyed working on a variety of related projects before I joining CMU, including experimental and theoretical sides.

Theoretical:

I received an undergraduate scholarship from Academia Sinica, Institute of Astronomy and Astrophysics, and had the priviledge of being advised by Prof. Hiroyuki Hirashita in developing a computational model to reveal the properties of high-redshift galaxy dust.

Experimental:

I worked on cryogenic systems and active vibration isolation systems for gravitational wave detection in LIGO (The Laser Interferometer Gravitational-Wave Observatory) group, under the guidance of Prof. Henry Tsz-King Wong and Prof. Yuki Inoue at Academia Sinica, Institute of Physics.

I earned my B.S. in Mechanical Engineering at National Chung Hsing University, Taiwan. My undergraduate project is building microdevices for liquid-liquid extractor, advised by Prof. Ya-Yu Chiang.
