Welcome to my academic page!

I am a second-year Biomedical Engineering master student at Carnegie Mellon University (CMU) advised by Prof. Tai-Sing Lee in the computer science department. I am passionate about uncovering the fundamental principle/mechanism behind complex systems, particularly in neuroscience. My interests lie in exploring the theoretical and computational principles of neural dynamics and population neural coding. Aside from that, I also have a strong affinity for physics and mathematics. My ultimate goal is to harmonize these diverse disciplines to address challenges of neural complex systems. Currently, I am actively seeking a computational neuroscience PhD position for the fall of 2024.

I currently works on two projects, including neural data analysis and building computational models. (1) Exploring the neural circuit plasticity in the early visual cortex as a result of familiarity learning with video stimulus. This research seeks to answer whether neural connectivity in the early visual cortex can encode spatial temporal memories with horizontal connections, leading to familiarity suppression. (2) Human emotion and music perception with EEG analysis. This research aims to explore the relationship between human's emotion and their perception of music by analysis of EEG data and constructing a Markov model.

Before joining CMU, I engaged in several physics-related research projects, including experimental side and theoretical side. I worked on cryogenic systems and active vibration isolation systems for gravitational wave detection in LIGO (The Laser Interferometer Gravitational-Wave Observatory) group, under the guidance of Prof. Henry Tsz-King Wong and Prof. Yuki Inoue at Academia Sinica, Institute of Physics. Additionally, I recerived an undergraduate scholarship from Academia Sinica, Institute of Astronomy and Astrophysics, and had the priviledge of being advised by Prof. Hiroyuki Hirashita in developing a computational model to reveal the properties of high-redshift galaxy dust.

I earned my B.S. in Mechanical Engineering at National Chung Hsing University, Taiwan. My undergraduate project is building micro devices for liquid-liquid extractor, advised by Prof. Ya-Yu Chiang.
